NDIS Yarn

Getting the right support for your NDIS service

NDIS Yarn is part of CheckUP’s NDIS Business Connect service. We offer practical business support for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander providers, businesses, and organisations navigating the NDIS.

Whether you are just starting out, growing your service, or getting ready for the 2026 NDIS changes, we can help you find the right tools, information, and support for your next step.

Why NDIS Yarn

Support that respects your lead

We know the best disability support for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities comes from providers who know their local area, community, and culture.

NDIS Yarn is here to support the business side of running an NDIS service, including planning, systems, staffing, and understanding NDIS requirements. That means you can stay focused on what matters most: supporting people and community.

Trusted entry point Practical support for providers at different stages of the NDIS journey.

Trust and transparency

A respectful first contact matters

We know trust matters. The person who first responds to your enquiry may not always be Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander, but they will work to listen respectfully, understand what you need, and connect you with the right support. CheckUP is also recruiting an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander-identified Project Manager to help shape and lead NDIS Yarn.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions

Do I have to be registered?

No. We support everyone from people with a brand new idea through to registered organisations.

Is this service free?

Yes. NDIS Yarn is a free service provided by CheckUP to help strengthen local NDIS providers.

Can you help with the 2026 NDIS changes?

Yes. We can help you understand updated arrangements and what they may mean for your business.

Will I be speaking with an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander staff member?

The person who first responds to your enquiry may not always be Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander. We want to be open about that. Our focus is on making sure your experience is respectful, helpful, and culturally aware, and we are currently recruiting an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander-identified Project Manager to help shape and lead this work.
